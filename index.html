Program Structure

Three tracks. One standard.

From AMC 8 through AIME, the program meets students where they are — grades 6 through 12 — and builds toward the next level.

Track A · Morning (AM)
AMC 8 Prep
Foundational competition math for middle schoolers. Covers all AMC 8 topic areas with emphasis on problem-solving instinct, arithmetic mastery, and contest strategy.
For grades 6–8 · No prior experience required
Track B · Afternoon
AMC 10/12 Foundations
Systematic coverage of all AMC 10/12 topic areas with an emphasis on speed, technique, and problem recognition. Designed to build the pattern library every top scorer relies on.
Target: AMC score 90 → 115+ · AMC score 75+ recommended
Track C · Evening (PM)
AMC 10/12 / AIME Bridge
Advanced material bridging AMC 10/12 and early AIME difficulty. Covers deeper techniques — modular arithmetic, complex number geometry, generating functions intro, and proof-based reasoning.
Target: AMC distinction → AIME qualification
Curriculum

Six weeks. Every major topic.

Each track runs its own independent six-week curriculum, calibrated to its level.

Track A — AMC 8 Prep Morning sessions · Grades 6–8
Week 01
Arithmetic & Algebra
Fractions, ratios, percents, basic equations, word problems, order of operations, intro to variables
Week 02
Number Theory
Divisibility, factors & multiples, LCM/GCD, prime factorization, basic modular arithmetic, digit problems
Week 03
Counting & Probability
Counting principles, permutations & combinations, basic probability, complementary counting, organized listing
Week 04
Geometry
Area & perimeter, angle relationships, similar figures, Pythagorean theorem, basic coordinate geometry
Week 05
Mixed Problem Solving
Multi-step problems, logic puzzles, pattern recognition, casework, building contest intuition across all topics
Week 06
Mock Contests & Strategy
Full AMC 8 simulations, pacing strategy, guessing strategy, post-contest review and targeted practice
Track B — AMC 10/12 Foundations Afternoon sessions
Week 01
Algebra Fundamentals
Factoring, Vieta's formulas, quadratic techniques, systems of equations, algebraic manipulation under time pressure
Week 02
Number Theory
Divisibility rules, LCM/GCD, prime factorization, basic modular arithmetic, digit problems
Week 03
Combinatorics
Counting principles, permutations & combinations, stars & bars, basic inclusion-exclusion, Pigeonhole principle
Week 04
Geometry
Angle chasing, similar triangles, area methods, basic coordinate geometry, circle theorems
Week 05
Probability & Counting
Basic probability, expected value, geometric probability, complementary counting, casework strategies
Week 06
Mock Contests & Strategy
Full AMC 10/12 simulations, time management, problem triage, post-contest error analysis
Track C — AMC 10/12 / AIME Bridge Evening sessions
Week 01
Advanced Algebra
AM-GM, Cauchy-Schwarz, polynomial roots & construction, functional equations, Vieta jumping
Week 02
Number Theory
Fermat's little theorem, Euler's totient, Chinese Remainder Theorem, lifting the exponent, quadratic residues, order of elements
Week 03
Advanced Combinatorics
Bijections, recursion & generating functions intro, inclusion-exclusion at depth, combinatorial identities, double counting
Week 04
Geometry
Power of a Point, radical axes, coordinate bash, trigonometric identities, Ptolemy's theorem, projective methods
Week 05
Probability & Combinatorics II
Advanced expected value, Markov chains intro, generating functions for probability, stars & bars extensions, complex counting arguments
Week 06
Mock Contests & Strategy
Full AMC 10/12 and AIME simulations, AIME-specific strategies, score optimization, targeted weakness review
